Client Experience Enhancement

In my role as a Virtual Customer Relations Officer for Yahweh Yasad Nursing Home, I recently had the opportunity to demonstrate the power of personalized care. A client expressed a heartfelt concern regarding his mother's home care services. The client's mother, battling dementia, received care from a nurse who worked standard business hours. The client requested the nurse cook meals and extend their service hours

My approach began with active listening. I engaged in empathetic conversations with the client, truly understanding their perspective and the emotional weight of caring for a parent with dementia.

Through collaboration with the head nurse, we identified areas for improvement within the existing framework. While the contract clearly stated nurses aren't authorized to cook, we explored alternative solutions. We offered to hire a chef specifically for meal preparation, ensuring the client's mother received proper nutrition.

I maintained regular communication with the client throughout the process. By explaining these alternatives and highlighting the contract limitations, we reached a compromise. A chef was hired to prepare meals, and the client felt a sense of peace knowing their mother's needs were addressed.

The client's subsequent testimonial reflects the impact of personalized care: "My mother, Mrs. Adekola Tokunbo, received excellent care from Yahweh Yasad. The quality of service was top-notch, and we found great value for the price. We’d choose them again."

This experience highlights the impact a virtual assistant. By actively listening, taking swift action, and providing personalized communication, I was able to navigate a complex situation and achieve a positive outcome.
